AI-for-Science Startup ChemLex Raises $45M for Drug Discovery Lab

Summary: AI-for-science startup ChemLex secured $45 million in funding to launch a self-driving laboratory in Singapore aimed at radically accelerating chemical discovery for the pharmaceutical industry.

Why it matters: This investment accelerates the shift toward fully automated R&D, meaning pharmaceutical companies can develop new drugs and chemical compounds significantly faster and at a lower cost, potentially bringing life-saving medicines to market years sooner.

President Trump to Sign Executive Order Creating Single National AI Rule

Summary: U.S. President Donald Trump announced plans to sign an executive order this week to establish a single, national regulatory framework for AI approval processes.

Why it matters: A unified national rule could streamline AI development by replacing a patchwork of state and local regulations, but it also raises questions about whether a single standard can adequately address the diverse risks of rapidly evolving AI technology.

Warby Parker and Google Partner to Launch AI-Powered Smart Glasses

Summary: Warby Parker and Google are collaborating to develop lightweight, AI-powered smart glasses, with the first consumer product expected to launch in 2026.

Why it matters: This partnership signals a serious push to make AI-integrated wearables mainstream, potentially moving smart glasses from a niche gadget to an everyday accessory that offers real-time, context-aware assistance.

Google's Gemini 3 Pro Now Available in CLI for Ultra Subscribers

Summary: Google has made its powerful Gemini 3 Pro model accessible via the command-line interface (CLI) for users with a Google AI Ultra subscription, expanding its use beyond the web interface.

Why it matters: This move allows developers and power users to integrate Google's most advanced AI model directly into their scripts and workflows, making complex, multi-step AI tasks faster and more seamless than ever before.

Comscore Releases 2025 AI Intelligence Report Highlighting Widespread Assistant Usage

Summary: Comscore's 2025 AI Intelligence Report found that AI assistant tools, such as ChatGPT, Gemini, and Copilot, are now used by 36% of desktop users and 24% of mobile users.

Why it matters: These statistics confirm that AI is no longer a niche technology but a core part of the daily digital experience for millions, making basic AI literacy an increasingly essential skill for work and life.
