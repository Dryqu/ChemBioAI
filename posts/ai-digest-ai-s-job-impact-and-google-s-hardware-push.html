AI Digest — AI's Job Impact and Google's Hardware Push

November 27, 2025 • By Yi Qu

MIT report: AI can already replace nearly 12% of the U.S. workforce

A new MIT study estimates that current AI systems are technically and economically capable of performing tasks equivalent to 11.7% of the U.S. labor market, representing about $1.2 trillion in wages.

**Why it matters:** This figure, which focuses on current economic feasibility rather than theoretical exposure, signals that the window for businesses and policymakers to prepare for massive workforce shifts is rapidly closing.

How Google put together the pieces for its AI comeback

Google's stock surged following the release of its latest AI model, Gemini 3, and its seventh-generation custom AI chip, Ironwood, which together are seen as a powerful, integrated AI stack.

**Why it matters:** This move intensifies the hardware race, showing that tech giants are not just competing on software models but also on custom silicon, which could lead to faster, more efficient AI for everyone.

Alibaba starts selling Quark AI glasses in China, enters global wearables race

Alibaba has launched its Quark AI glasses in China, joining companies like Meta and Amazon in the push to integrate AI assistants into consumer-facing smart eyewear.

**Why it matters:** The increasing number of tech giants releasing AI glasses suggests that the next major platform shift may be from smartphones to smart wearables, making AI a constant, hands-free companion.

What bubble? The analysts and investors making the bull case for AI

Despite some market volatility, analysts and investors are maintaining a strong "bull case" for AI, citing massive enterprise adoption and the foundational nature of the technology as reasons for continued growth.

**Why it matters:** This ongoing debate affects investment decisions across the economy, as the belief that AI is a fundamental, long-term shift—not a temporary bubble—drives trillions of dollars in capital allocation.
