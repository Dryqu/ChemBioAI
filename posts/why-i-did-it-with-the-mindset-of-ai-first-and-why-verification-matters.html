Why I Did It with the Mindset of “AI First” (and Why Verification Matters)

December 09, 2025 • By Yi Qu

I got a request to complete a spreadsheet, but the information was scattered across many long reports. Instead of spending hours reading and copying, I approached it with the mindset of “AI first.”

I used Microsoft Copilot to upload all the documents including the spreadsheet and asked it to extract the details I needed, organize them in the order of my spreadsheet, and even auto-fill it. Then I added one critical instruction: include the file name and page number for every single value. That simple step saved me a huge amount of time because when it came to verification, I didn’t have to search — I could go straight to the source in seconds.

The result? What used to take half a day was done in minutes. But here’s the most important part: verification is non-negotiable. AI can speed up your work, but accuracy is your responsibility. Always check the sources. Always confirm the numbers. AI is your assistant, not your final authority.

If you adopt an “AI-first” mindset for repetitive tasks, you’ll save time, reduce errors, and still maintain control over quality. And if you’re wondering which tool to start with, I recommend Microsoft Copilot for its integration with Office apps and strong source-tracking capability — but any reliable AI tool that supports source references can work.
