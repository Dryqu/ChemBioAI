A Simple AI Tip: Use Built-In OCR Instead of Installing Your Own

December 10, 2025 • By Yi Qu

When we think about AI, we usually think about:

  • Searching for information
  • Generating text, images, or videos

But there is another very practical use:

Using AI tools as a built-in OCR engine.

OCR (Optical Character Recognition) means turning a picture or PDF with text into real, editable text. Today, many AI assistants (e.g. Copilot) already include powerful OCR inside. You don't always need to install extra software.

The "old way": installing your own OCR

In the past, if I wanted to use OCR, I had to download and install tools like Tesseract on my computer.

This tool can work, but:

  • It is sometimes tricky to set up
  • The quality is not always stable

There are also some advanced OCR tools, such as DeepSeek OCR. They are very powerful, but they use a lot of computer resources and work best with a GPU. If your computer is not powerful, they can be quite slow.

The "new way": let AI assistants do the OCR

Now, I often skip local OCR tools.

Instead, I use AI assistants with built-in OCR. The workflow is very simple:

  1. Upload the PDF or image to the AI assistant
  2. Ask something like:
"Pleased extract all the text from this PDF and give me a clean, downloadable file."

The AI runs OCR in the background and returns the text. I can then copy, edit, or download the result.

No need to worry about installation, drivers, or hardware.

Why this matters

This small trick is helpful if you:

  • Work with scanned PDFs
  • Take photos of documents (notes, contracts, lab data, receipts, etc.)
  • Need to quickly turn paper into searchable digital text

So next time you open an AI tool, don't think of it only as a "chatbot."

Think of it as a multi-tool that already includes OCR - fast, powerful, and ready to use.
