AI's Global Impact: Jobs, Chips, and Molecular Design

November 27, 2025

1. MIT Study Finds AI Can Already Replace 11.7% of U.S. Workforce

A new study from MIT and Oak Ridge National Laboratory, using a labor simulation tool called the Iceberg Index, found that current AI systems could already perform tasks equivalent to 11.7% of the U.S. labor market, or $1.2 trillion in wages.

Why it matters: This shows that the impact of AI extends far beyond the visible tech layoffs, affecting routine administrative and financial roles across all 50 states, giving policymakers a critical tool to prepare for widespread economic shifts.

2. Google, the sleeping giant in global AI race, now ‘fully awake’

Google's new Gemini 3 Pro model has earned immediate praise for its superior reasoning and coding capabilities, signaling a major resurgence in the company's competition with rivals like OpenAI.

Why it matters: The launch of a new "tier 1 LLM" from a major player intensifies the AI arms race, pushing the boundaries of what generative models can achieve in complex problem-solving and enterprise applications.

3. A virtual platform for automated hybrid organic-enzymatic synthesis planning

Researchers have developed ChemEnzyRetroPlanner, an open-source, AI-driven platform that automatically plans complex chemical synthesis routes by combining traditional organic chemistry with highly selective enzymatic reactions.

Why it matters: For pharmaceutical R&D and industrial chemistry, this tool can significantly accelerate the development of new molecules by automating the design of more efficient, cost-effective, and environmentally sustainable manufacturing processes.

4. Meta Is in Talks to Use Google's Chips in Challenge to Nvidia

Meta is reportedly in talks with Google to purchase its custom Tensor Processing Units (TPUs) for its AI infrastructure, a move that would reduce its reliance on Nvidia's dominant GPU hardware.

Why it matters: This potential multi-billion dollar deal highlights the growing strategic importance of specialized AI chips and signals a major effort by tech giants to diversify their supply chains and challenge Nvidia's near-monopoly in the AI hardware market.

5. Perplexity Adds AI-Powered Shopping Feature With PayPal Checkout

The AI search engine Perplexity has launched a new personalized shopping experience that uses conversational AI to suggest products and allows for direct checkout via PayPal with supported merchants.

Why it matters: As AI models move into e-commerce, this feature could fundamentally change how people shop by prioritizing user history and preferences over traditional search engine optimization, potentially leading to a more tailored and efficient buying experience.

6. 37 State Attorneys General Defend States' Right to Regulate AI

A coalition of 37 state Attorneys General signed a letter to Congress urging them to preserve the rights of states to enact their own regulations on artificial intelligence, amid talks of a potential federal moratorium.

Why it matters: The debate over whether AI should be regulated at the state or federal level is critical, as state-level laws could lead to a patchwork of rules that affect how AI companies operate and how consumers are protected across the country.
