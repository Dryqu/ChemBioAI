Dawn Song, Berkeley RDI, and the Rise of Agentic AI Education

December 17, 2025 • By Yi Qu

A year ago, “agentic AI” was still insider language.In 2025, it became the fastest-moving layer of the AI stack—because it turns models into systems that can actually get work done.

One of the people who pushed that shift early: Professor Dawn Song at UC Berkeley.

From Models to Agents: The Shift That Defined 2025

Large language models were impressive long before 2025. But what pushed AI into a new phase was agency: systems that don’t just respond, but reason, plan, and act across tools, environments, and time.

This shift unlocked real automation—coding agents, scientific discovery workflows, multi-agent simulations, and embodied systems that learn through interaction. It also surfaced new challenges: evaluation, safety, predictability, and security at scale.

Anticipating this transition early, Berkeley RDI launched its first Agentic AI MOOC in Fall 2024. At the time, it felt forward-looking. One year later, the field caught up.

Education at the Speed of the Field

By Fall 2025, agentic AI education had to evolve fast.

The Agentic AI MOOC builds on earlier courses in LLM agents and advanced agent systems, but shifts the focus from concepts to systems thinking: how agents are trained, evaluated, deployed, and governed in the real world.

The Fall 2025 syllabus reflects where the field actually is (https://agenticai-learning.org/f25):

  • Post-training and verifiable agents
  • Multi-agent coordination
  • Evaluation benchmarks and uncertainty
  • Deployment lessons from production systems
  • Safety and security risks unique to autonomous agents

This isn’t theory-heavy coursework. It’s a map of what breaks first when agents leave the lab.

Research, Security, and the Cost of Capability

As agents became more capable, another reality became impossible to ignore: security and misuse risks scale with autonomy.

Berkeley RDI’s work in frontier AI cybersecurity—through projects like CyberGym and BountyBench—demonstrated how AI systems can already uncover real-world vulnerabilities at scale. That power cuts both ways.

The launch of the Frontier AI Cybersecurity Observatory reflects a growing consensus: monitoring AI capability is no longer optional. Responsible progress requires continuous measurement, not one-time evaluations.

Why This Moment Matters

2025 will be remembered as the year AI agents went mainstream—not because of hype, but because they started doing real work.

Education, research, and community-building now have to move together:

  • Education to create fluent builders, not prompt users
  • Research to surface limitations early
  • Community to share failures as openly as successes

Agentic AI isn’t just another abstraction layer. It’s a shift in how intelligence gets applied.

Thank You—and a Recommendation
